The Problem

The Invisible
Hormone Problem

Cortisol is the single most influential hormone in daily human functioning. It regulates energy, immunity, metabolism, sleep, mood, and stress response. Every system in your body listens to cortisol.

Yet it is the least monitored hormone in medicine.

The Gap

Snapshots Miss the Pattern

Cortisol disorders are pattern disorders. The question is never "is your cortisol high?" — it's "is it high at the wrong time, for too long, or not rising when it should?" A single lab test cannot answer that. Only a continuous curve can.

Blood Draw

Single snapshot. Requires clinic visit. 8 AM timing misses afternoon and evening dysregulation.

24-Hour Urine

One average over a full day. Loses all timing information. Inconvenient and often incomplete.

Saliva Kit

Mailed to a lab. Results in 1-2 weeks. Single data point per collection. Impractical for daily tracking.

CortiPod

Readings every 15 minutes. Full diurnal curve. On your wrist. Data on your phone. Every day.

Clinical Conditions — 01

Cushing's Syndrome

The body produces too much cortisol. Causes progressive weight gain, muscle wasting, hypertension, diabetes, osteoporosis, and psychiatric symptoms.

Average time to diagnosis: 3-6 years. Patients are told they're stressed, eating too much, or aging.

CortiPod value: Continuous diurnal curve reveals loss of normal rhythm — the hallmark of Cushing's. Could shorten diagnostic odyssey from years to weeks.

Prevalence — North America

20-26K

diagnosed cases in the US. True incidence may be 2-3x higher than reported.

Current Monitoring

Single-point tests: morning blood, 24-hr urine, late-night saliva, dexamethasone suppression. Each is a snapshot. Cyclic Cushing's evades all of them.

Clinical Conditions — 02

Addison's Disease

Adrenal glands cannot produce sufficient cortisol. Fatal without treatment. Patients face chronic fatigue, dangerously low blood pressure, and constant risk of adrenal crisis.

Often not diagnosed until patient presents in crisis — hypotensive, confused, critically ill.

CortiPod value: Diagnosed patients can optimize replacement dosing. Undiagnosed patients see a flat curve with no morning peak — a signal to seek evaluation before crisis.

Prevalence — North America

13-20K

in the US. 40-60 per million. 90% autoimmune in origin.

Current Monitoring

Morning serum cortisol + ACTH stimulation test. On replacement therapy, no way to verify dosing produces a normal daily curve.

Clinical Conditions — 03

Steroid-Induced Adrenal
Insufficiency

Long-term corticosteroid use shuts down natural cortisol production. When steroids are tapered, adrenal glands need 6-12 months to restart. Recovery is unpredictable and unmonitored.

This is not rare. 2.5% of the US population is on chronic corticosteroids.

CortiPod value: Track the return of natural cortisol production in real time. Guide taper speed with data. The largest single clinical population in this analysis.

Prevalence — North America

3-4M

people in the US with steroid-induced adrenal suppression. 37-49% of the ~8M chronic steroid users.

Current Monitoring

Periodic ACTH stimulation tests during taper — invasive, clinic-based, months apart. No home monitoring exists.

Clinical Conditions — 04

Congenital Adrenal
Hyperplasia

Inherited genetic disorders affecting cortisol synthesis. Classic form detected at birth. Non-classic form (NCCAH) is far more common, milder, and frequently undiagnosed.

Children require lifelong replacement with frequent dose adjustments during growth. Under- and over-treatment both carry serious consequences.

CortiPod value: Continuous cortisol tracking helps parents and endocrinologists optimize pediatric dosing between clinic visits without repeated blood draws.

Prevalence — North America

22K+

classic CAH in the US. Non-classic: 1 in 200-1,000 depending on ethnicity — potentially hundreds of thousands undiagnosed.

Current Monitoring

Serum blood draws every 3-6 months. Between visits, parents and patients rely on subjective symptom tracking.

Clinical Conditions — 05

Chronic Stress &
Burnout

Prolonged HPA axis activation leads to cortisol dysregulation: flattened diurnal curve, elevated evening cortisol, blunted morning response. A documented precursor to depression, cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, and immune dysfunction.

The most prevalent health risk factor in North America, with no standard objective measurement.

CortiPod value: The primary consumer use case. Makes stress visible and measurable. Track lifestyle interventions with real data, not guesswork.

Prevalence — North America

150M+

US adults report stress affects their health. 55% of workforce experiencing burnout. 72% face moderate-to-high work stress.

Economic Impact

$190B in US stress-related healthcare (2025). $1T global lost productivity (WHO).

Clinical Conditions — 06

HPA Axis Dysfunction

Cortisol produced at wrong times, in wrong amounts, or failing to respond to stress. Documented in major depression (40-60% of patients), PTSD, anxiety disorders, and chronic fatigue.

Strong research evidence links cortisol rhythm disruption to psychiatric outcomes — yet cortisol is almost never measured in psychiatric care.

CortiPod value: A biomarker companion to mental health treatment. Objective signal of biological progress during therapy or medication adjustment.

Prevalence — North America

8-13M

with depression-related HPA dysfunction. PTSD: ~13M. Anxiety: ~40M. Conservatively, tens of millions.

Current Monitoring

Cortisol is rarely measured in psychiatric settings. When measured: single morning blood draw. Inadequate for rhythm characterization.

Clinical Conditions — 07

Shift Work &
Circadian Disruption

Night shifts, rotating schedules, and chronic jet lag disrupt cortisol rhythm. IARC classifies night shift work as a Group 2A probable carcinogen. Linked to cardiovascular disease, diabetes, obesity, depression, and cancer.

A structural feature of the modern economy affecting healthcare workers, first responders, factory workers, and service employees.

CortiPod value: See how schedules affect cortisol. Track whether countermeasures (light therapy, meal timing, strategic napping) are restoring a healthier pattern.

Prevalence — North America

16M

Americans work non-daytime shifts. 10-40% develop Shift Work Disorder. ~20% of the global workforce.

Current Monitoring

Sleep studies and actigraphy assess sleep quality but not hormonal consequence. No consumer cortisol tool exists for shift workers.

Consumer Value Proposition

Is This You?

Seven profiles. Same invisible problem. Real people who could finally see what their body has been trying to tell them.

Consumer — 01
"You've been gaining weight and no one can tell you why"

What might be happening

Your body may be producing too much of its primary stress hormone — and a single morning blood test can miss it entirely. A level that looks fine at 8 AM may be wrong at 10 PM.

How CortiPod helps

Track your cortisol across the full day. If levels stay elevated when they should drop, you'll have real data to bring to your doctor — not just symptoms they can't see.

Consumer — 02
"You're exhausted no matter how much you rest"

What might be happening

Your cortisol production may be lower than it should be. The key clue is in the daily pattern — not just whether cortisol is present, but whether it rises and falls the way it should.

How CortiPod helps

A healthy curve peaks in the morning and tapers through the day. If yours is flat — low all day — that's a pattern worth showing your doctor. Continuous data reveals what a single blood draw cannot.

Consumer — 03
"You're tapering off steroids and don't know if your body is recovering"

What might be happening

Long-term steroid use suppresses your body's natural cortisol production. When you taper, your adrenal glands need 6-12 months to restart. Millions go through this every year with no way to see what's happening.

How CortiPod helps

Watch your natural cortisol return in real time. As your body recovers, you'll see the morning peak reappear. Share the trend with your doctor to guide taper speed. Real data instead of waiting and hoping.

Consumer — 04
"You feel like you're running on empty and burning out"

What might be happening

Chronic stress changes your cortisol pattern invisibly. Your curve may be flat, elevated at night, or barely rising in the morning. You can't feel this shift — it shows up as insomnia, weight gain, brain fog, or getting sick more often.

How CortiPod helps

See your stress hormone in real numbers — not a score derived from heart rate. Track whether the changes you're making produce a measurable result. Replace guesswork with data. Know what's working.

Consumer — 05
"You're working on your mental health, but can't tell if it's working"

What might be happening

Up to 60% of people experiencing depression have a disrupted cortisol rhythm. As treatment works, that rhythm often normalizes — but no one is tracking it. The biological shift can precede the emotional one.

How CortiPod helps

An objective biomarker alongside your subjective experience. If your curve is normalizing over weeks, that's a real signal. Something concrete to discuss with your clinician. Not a replacement — a companion.

Consumer — 06
"You work nights and feel like your body never adjusts"

What might be happening

Your cortisol rhythm is likely inverted or flattened. Your body produces the wake-up hormone when you're trying to sleep. Over months and years, this misalignment is linked to serious health consequences.

How CortiPod helps

See exactly how your schedule affects your cortisol cycle. Track whether strategies like timed light exposure or meal timing are helping. Your shift may not change, but how your body handles it can.
