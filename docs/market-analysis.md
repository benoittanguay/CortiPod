- Premium to Oura's ongoing subscription ($70/yr) but Oura doesn't measure biomarkers from body fluids
- Be accessible to the target demographic (HH income $100K+)

### Indirect Competitors (Stress Monitoring via Proxy)

| Company | Product | Method | Limitation |
|---------|---------|--------|------------|
| **Whoop** | Whoop 5.0 | HRV, skin temp, SpO2 | No direct cortisol measurement — infers stress from HRV |
| **Oura** | Ring Gen 4 | HRV, skin temp | Same — stress scores derived from HRV proxy |
| **Apple** | Watch Ultra | HRV | Same |
| **Garmin** | Various | HRV, Body Battery | Same |

**Key differentiator:** Every existing wearable infers stress from heart rate variability (HRV), which is an indirect proxy with significant noise. CortiPod would be the first wrist-worn device to measure cortisol directly from sweat — the actual stress hormone.

## Key Market Signals

1. **76% of U.S. adults say stress hurts their health, yet zero consumer wearables directly measure cortisol today.** The gap between consumer demand and available measurement technology is enormous.

2. **The premium health wearable market is proven and accelerating.** Whoop doubled to $1.1B revenue run rate; Oura doubled to ~$1B. Both valued at $10B+. Consumers will pay $200-500/yr for invisible biomarker data.

3. **Cortisol is already the leading segment** in the wearable sweat sensor market by revenue share — validating that this is where science and consumer demand intersect.

4. **The wellness CGM parallel is the strongest comparison.** Dexcom Stelo hit $100M in year one selling glucose data to non-diabetics. Cortisol monitoring for stress is a broader use case than glucose monitoring for metabolic health.

5. **Direct competition is nascent.** EnLiSense/Corti is the closest wearable competitor but hasn't publicly launched with pricing. ELI Health is saliva-based, not continuous or wearable. The field is wide open.

6. **Corporate wellness is a strong B2B channel.** Stress management ($9.1B globally) is the fastest-growing segment in corporate wellness — a natural enterprise sales motion alongside DTC.

7. **Credit card companies are subsidizing health wearables.** AmEx Platinum added a $200 Oura credit; Chase Sapphire Reserve added a $359 Whoop rebate. This signals mainstream affluent consumer adoption and willingness to pay.
